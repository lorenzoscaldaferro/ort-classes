Let's take a closer look at all the types. Now-next-later. This roadmap categorizes tasks by priority, with "now" indicating immediate tasks, "next" being short-term objectives, and "later" for long-term goals. This approach keeps your team focused on the most important tasks at hand. Feature-based. This type of roadmap focuses on individual features and their release dates. It's particularly useful for development teams, as it highlights which features need to be completed by when. Here's an example where all the features are sorted according to the quarters, which makes it easy to understand how different product elements are going to evolve with time. Goal-based. Goal-based roadmaps prioritize company-wide objectives and link them to specific product initiatives. This approach is great for aligning your product with your organization's long-term vision. Strategy. Strategic roadmaps focus on high-level product goals and initiatives. This type is particularly helpful for communicating with stakeholders and executives, ensuring everyone is aligned on the overall product direction. Release. Release roadmaps provide an overview of planned releases and their corresponding features. This type is useful for coordinating work across different teams and departments, ensuring everyone is aligned on what's coming up.
